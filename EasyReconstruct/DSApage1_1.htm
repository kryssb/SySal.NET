Decay Search Step #1.1

Definition of the Main Vertex (Step 1 of the reference document)

Do for me
SBSF/TSR Pos. tol.
SBSF/TSR Slope tol.

In this step you are required to find the "main vertex": this is usually the primary one, but may be a secondary vertex produced in a short τ decay.
The procedure to obtain this depends on whether you arrived at the vertex along a Scanback path or not.
In case of Scanback, do the following steps:
  1. Select SBSF and CS data sets, deselect all others
  2. Select "Tracks" on the main panel and click "Plot"
  3. Locate the SB track whose stopping point triggered the TotalScan and click on it
  4. Go to the "Neighborhood" tab, and select "Matching tracks from other datasets" (you can adjust DeltaSlope and Radius if you wish)
  5. Among the TSR-type tracks, choose the one that is closer to the stopping point; if no track appears, or if the automatic matching is not satisfactory, switch to the procedure for no Scanback track.
  6. If the track selected has a vertex attached, this is a good candidate for being the main vertex; if no vertex is attached, the event might have a single prong (which is often the case for QE-like events), or other tracks might have been missed.
In the case where the probable interaction point was not located using Scanback, do the following steps:
  1. Select TSR and CS datasets, deselect all others
  2. Select "Segments" on the main panel and click "Plot"
  3. Look for tracks with specific slopes, using the following search strings (one after the other until something reasonable is obtained):
    • sqrt((XSLOPE-sx)^2+(YSLOPE-sy)^2)<0.03 && UVID >= 0    (select segments of tracks with a certain slope attached to vertices upstream - replace XSLOPE and YSLOPE with your values)
    • sqrt((XSLOPE-sx)^2+(YSLOPE-sy)^2)<0.03 && UVID >= 3    (select segments of tracks with a certain sleop and at least 3 segments - replace XSLOPE and YSLOPE with your values)
    • sqrt((XSLOPE-sx)^2+(YSLOPE-sy)^2)<0.03    (select any segments with a certain slope - replace XSLOPE and YSLOPE with your values)
  4. Choose segments that may be interesting, and browse the related track (promote single segments to tracks if needed)
